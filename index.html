Program Information:


  1. DSGN270 - Web Design Theory

    • Responsive Websites
      • Content that responds to screen size and orientation.
      • See all information on all devices.
      • Using different layouts for smaller screens.
      • Design for small screens and then expand.
      • Get creative with menus and navigation.
    • User Experience Design
      • Designing to limit user frustration.
      • Solve the users problem.
      • Dont flood your page with info, be concise.
      • People like pictures and bullet points.
      • Design for a novice, expect no experience from them.
      • Dont change things too radically or uses might get lost.
    • Accessibility and Disablity
      • Developing with physical and mental disabilies in mind.
      • Correct use of headings and alternate text for assistive technologies.
      • Accesiblity can sometimes create new features for regular users.
    • Copyright, Information Architecture
      • Canadian origional content is automatically protected.
      • Registered copyright certificates can help you in court.
      • Exists for 50 years following your death.
      • When it expires your work becomes public domain.
      • Helps protect value and branding.

  2. CPNT260 - Web Page Construction Fundamentals

    • Wireframing and Layout
    • Content Strategy
      • Itallics, boldm underline.
      • Correct headings for SEO.
      • "em" and "cite" are different!
    • Write Better
      • Identify and describe target audience.
      • Structure the content to guide the reader.
      • Write content in plain, concise language.
      • Information design helps readers understand.
      • Work with the target user groups to test and revise.
      • Remove what's unnecessary - don't flood the user with content.
      • No walls of text - information in micro formats.
      • Microcontent example: 404 Page Not Found, Twitter.
      • Headings, links, form labels, navigation items, etc. are also microcontent.
      • Be unique with your 404 Page Not Found - github, mailchimp.
      • Humor makes 404’s less interruptive but only when appropriate.
      • Split up long paragraphs - max 4 sentences.
      • Make it “scannable” - different headings and icons.
      • Reader won't read everything - bullet points very effective.
      • Use headings - top, second, third level(assistive technology).
      • Use stylised lists, you don't always need numbers or bullet points
      • “What is it”, “how it works”, “who is it for” - break it down.
      • Get them to what they're looking for faster.
    • Semantic Markup
      • Itallics, boldm underline.
      • Correct headings for SEO.
      • "em" and "cite" are different!
    • Content Inventory
      • Create a list of all content on the website when revising it.
        • Go through the list and decide what to keep.
        • Figure out what content needs to be created or changed.
        • Decide who is responsible for editing and developing each piece of content.
      • Common info to note:
        • URL's.
        • Page names.
        • Things to remember.
        • Relationship to other content.
      • Additional info:
        • Content type.
        • Descriptions.
        • Authors.
        • Owners.
        • Date updated.
        • Numering system.
        • Reviews, comments, responses.
